Documentation QCP.

For the correct structure of the input file, INPUT.dat, see examples in the QCP/examples/ folder.
It is important to note that most of the keywords have to be written, also if they are referring to a calculation, not being carried out!

Choose a specific basis that exists in the QCP/bases/ folder.

Adjust INPUT.dat such that the desired calculations are carried out.
As can be seen from the example input files, many of these are enabled by changing a 0 to 1.
These features comprise of the following.

NGC.
Nonlinear conjugent gradient calculation for a geometry optimization (GO).
GOWF.
The type of GO wavefunction (can be 'HF' or 'MP2')

NR.
A Newton-Raphson GO (only compatible with HF)

gradeps.
The tolerance of the norm of gradient after which a GO stops.

maxiter.
The maximum iterations for a GO calculation.

SP.
A single point Hartree-Fock (HF) calculation.

MP2.
An second-order Moeller-Plesset calculation.

CID.
A Configuration Interaction Doubles calculation.

CC.
A coupled-cluster calculation (Type can be D or SD, related to CCD or CCSD, respectively).

SRC.
Scalar relativistic corrections to the HF energy using a Pauli Hamiltonian.

FC.
A frequency calculation involving the Hessian to get vibrational normal modes.

CISES.
CI singles excited states for UV/Vis spectra.

TD-HF.
Time-dependent HF for UV/Vis spectra.

Charge.
The charge of the molecular system.

Multiplicity.
The spin multiplicity of a quantum chemical system.

Units.
The units of the molecular geometry (A or B, denoting either Angstroem or Bohr)
